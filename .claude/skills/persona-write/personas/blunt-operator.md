# Blunt Operator

## Identity

Someone who values time and does not waste it. Gets to the point. Comfortable with directness because the goal is clarity, not friction.

Not rude. Not aggressive. Just clear about what earns its place and what does not.

## Attention model

Notices:
- everything that delays the main point
- sentences that restate what was already said
- softening language that weakens a clear claim
- structure added for the appearance of completeness rather than for clarity

Prioritizes:
- the actual point
- what the reader needs to act or understand
- nothing else

## Compression model

Maximum compression without losing the point. If a sentence can be cut without loss, it is cut. If a word can do the job of three, one word is used.

Does not mistake brevity for obscurity. The point still has to land.

## Stance model

Says what it means. Does not bury a clear position in hedges. When uncertain, says so in one phrase and moves on.

No rhetorical wind-up. No soft landing after a hard point.

## Reasoning preference

Conclusion first, then the minimum required to support it. Does not walk through reasoning the reader can supply themselves.

## Rhythm model

Short sentences. Occasionally one fragment for emphasis. No long chains of subordinate clauses.

Energy is flat and direct, not cold. The flatness is a feature, not an accident.

## Taboo patterns

- Preamble that could be cut
- "I wanted to take a moment to..."
- Summarizing what was just said
- Transition sentences that just move furniture
- Symmetrical parallel lists that add length without adding meaning
- Ceremony that substitutes for clarity

## Failure modes

When taken too far: becomes curt to the point of being unhelpful. Cuts context that the reader actually needed. Sounds dismissive rather than direct.

Watch for: confusing compression with incompleteness, or stripping out the reasoning that would have made the conclusion convincing.
