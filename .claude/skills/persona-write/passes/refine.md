# Pass: Refine

## Purpose

Tighten and smooth the rewritten text after the anti-AI scrub.

The rewrite and scrub passes address major problems. The refine pass addresses what remains: awkward phrasing, uneven rhythm, sentences that work but could work better.

This is a lighter pass. Do not re-diagnose. Do not re-structure. Work at the sentence and paragraph level.

## What to do

### Tighten sentences
Are there words that are doing no work?

- Modifiers that do not change the meaning
- Prepositional phrases that can be cut
- Clauses that state the obvious

Cut them.

### Fix rhythm breaks
Read through the text for rhythm. Where does it stumble?

- Two long sentences in a row that need a short break
- A series of short sentences that reads like a list when it is not one
- A paragraph where every sentence is the same length

Adjust sentence length and structure to restore natural rhythm.

### Repair awkward transitions
Are there places where two ideas butt up against each other without connection?

Add the minimum needed to bridge them. Do not add a full transition sentence if a single word or phrase will do.

### Verify compression
Is anything still over-explained? Is anything under-explained?

The rewrite should have handled this, but check again. It is easy to introduce compression problems while fixing rhythm problems.

### Check persona consistency
Does the whole piece feel like the same person wrote it?

Places to check:
- The first sentence versus the last sentence
- Transitions between sections
- Any part that was difficult to rewrite and may have drifted

Restore persona consistency where it has drifted.

## What not to do

- Do not change meaning to improve prose flow. If a sentence is awkward because the idea is genuinely difficult, leave it and flag it.
- Do not add content. Refine is a cutting and smoothing pass, not an expansion pass.
- Do not over-refine. A piece that reads naturally is done. More polishing does not always improve it.

## Output

The refined text.

If the refine pass made minor adjustments, no annotation is needed unless the user requested a change summary.
