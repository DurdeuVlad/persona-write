# Manager Speak

Corporate and managerial language patterns that produce the appearance of communication without the substance.

These patterns appear in business writing, product documentation, team communications, and anywhere language has been optimized to sound competent rather than to convey meaning.

## Verb-to-noun inflation

Converting verbs into nouns drains energy from sentences and hides who is doing what.

| Inflated | Direct |
|---|---|
| make a decision | decide |
| provide an explanation | explain |
| conduct an analysis | analyze |
| offer a recommendation | recommend |
| achieve a reduction | reduce |
| implement a solution | fix / solve |
| perform an assessment | assess |
| facilitate alignment | align |

## Empty action words

These verbs feel active but carry no specific meaning.

- leverage
- utilize (instead of use)
- operationalize
- socialize (a document, an idea)
- align (as a verb in most contexts)
- drive (when not moving a vehicle)
- surface (as a verb: "surface the issue")
- unpack (as a verb: "let's unpack this")
- ideate
- action (as a verb: "action this item")

## Status theater

Phrases that signal effort without describing it.

- "We are continuing to monitor the situation..."
- "We remain committed to..."
- "We are actively working to..."
- "We are fully aligned on..."
- "We have taken this on board..."
- "This is a priority for the team..."
- "We are exploring options..."

## Passive responsibility avoidance

Using passive voice to avoid naming who made a decision or who is responsible for an action.

- "It was decided that..."
- "Mistakes were made..."
- "The project was deprioritized..."
- "This was escalated to..."
- "It has been determined that..."

## Impact vocabulary

Overused words that have been drained of meaning through business-writing inflation.

- impact (as a verb)
- synergy
- bandwidth (for human attention or capacity)
- visibility (meaning "awareness of")
- transparency (as a promise)
- scalable (applied to anything not a technical system)
- robust (when the actual reason for strength is not named)
- seamless

## Framing for audience comfort

Phrases that soften bad news or uncomfortable facts without changing them.

- "As we navigate this transition..."
- "In the spirit of continuous improvement..."
- "This presents an opportunity to..."
- "I am confident that..."
- "We are excited to share..."
- "This is a challenging but important moment..."
