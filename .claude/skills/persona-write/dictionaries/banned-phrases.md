# Banned Phrases

These phrases appear frequently in AI-generated writing and weaken the text. Avoid them in all persona modes unless there is a specific reason to use one.

This is not an exhaustive blocklist. It is a pattern guide. The problem is not the words themselves — it is what they signal: that the writer is stalling, hedging unnecessarily, or padding.

## Opener stalls

These phrases delay the point without adding anything.

- "It is worth noting that..."
- "It is important to note that..."
- "It should be noted that..."
- "One thing to keep in mind is..."
- "First and foremost..."
- "In today's world..."
- "In this day and age..."
- "When it comes to..."
- "With that in mind..."
- "That being said..."
- "Having said that..."
- "At the end of the day..."

## Fake precision

These phrases signal thoroughness without providing it.

- "There are many factors to consider..."
- "This is a complex issue..."
- "It depends on a variety of factors..."
- "Results may vary..."
- "In many cases..."
- "For many people..."
- "In some situations..."

## Enthusiasm performance

These phrases signal excitement the writer does not actually feel.

- "This is exciting..."
- "This is fascinating..."
- "What a great question..."
- "I'm thrilled to..."
- "It's incredible how..."
- "The amazing thing about..."

## Hollow transitions

These transitions exist only to move from one paragraph to the next.

- "Furthermore..."
- "Moreover..."
- "Additionally, it is important to..."
- "In conclusion..."
- "To summarize..."
- "As we have seen..."
- "In light of the above..."

## Softening non-starters

These phrases hedge a point into uselessness.

- "While it may be the case that..."
- "It could be argued that..."
- "One might suggest that..."
- "There is a possibility that..."
- "It is not unreasonable to think that..."
